Your mom's so stupid, when she was asked to Describe Your Mom Here ["https://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/JustForFun/DescribeTopicHere"], she said she didn't know your mom.
In a war of Volleying Insults ["https://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/Main/VolleyingInsults"], insulting the opponent's mother is a useful snappy comeback. After all, Even Bad Men Love Their Mamas, and going directly after their mother is a surefire way to strike a nerve in one's opponent.
In its most primitive form, the trope is a No, You ["https://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/Main/NoYou"] variant of the Lame Comeback, but with "your mom" substituted for "you" ("Your mom's a lame comeback!") The next step up is "That's what your mom said!", becoming a more explicit Double Entendre. But the minimum for an artfully done "Your Mom" joke (such as it is) is along the lines of "Your mom's so fat/stupid/old/slutty, she [insert joke here]", which lends itself very well to Volleying Insults, to the point that it's a staple of the genre, and a theme of the "Dirty Dozens" comedy game.
Other family members or significant others may be substituted (e.g. "your wife", "your old man"), but people are most likely to have good relationships with their mothers, so "your mom" is the most effective of them. A Berserk Button ["https://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/Main/BerserkButton"] for an Extremely Protective Child.
Of course, dialectical variants exist as well, from the ghetto-sounding "yo' mamma" to the Australian "ya mum", as used in the nation's prolific swearing. And as always, Wikipedia ["https://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/Website/Wikipedia"] provides much more detail, in its traditional deadpan way.
